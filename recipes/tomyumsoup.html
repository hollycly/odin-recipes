Tom Yum Soup

Description

In Khmer the word machu means “sour” and the word salaw means “soup”. Thus, this soup is also called “Salaw machu”. The soup is a mix of sweet, sour, and—maybe—spicy, if you so choose. The aromaticity of the soup can be attributed to a mix of kaffir lime leaves, galangal and lemongrass.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 pounds medium-sized whole raw shrimp with heads intact
  • 6 cups water
  • 4 lemongrass stalks
  • 2 inch piece galangal root
  • 16 Kaffir lime leaves
  • 1 cup pineapple
  • 1/2 onion, peeled and cut into wedges
  • 4-6 Thai chili (or Thai chili paste to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauch
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1/2 cup straw or button mushrooms, sliced (optional)
  • 2 limes

Garnishes:

  • cilantro leaves
  • lime wedges

Steps

  1. In a pot, add water and bring to a boil. Cut your lemongrass and galangal into large pieces. Peel and cut your onion wedges. Mince your Thai chili.
  2. Add in your lemongrass, galangal, and kaffir leaves to your boild water and let simmer.
  3. Add all of your remaining ingredients (except limes) and let all marry and simmer.
  4. You can fish out the aromatics such as the lemongrass and lime leaves if you'd like.
  5. At the end, add your fresh lime and garnishes and serve with a hot bowl of rice. Enjoy!

Tip

“I like to peel my shrimp but keep the head and tail on for extra flavor. But, it makes it easier when you are peeling and enjoying the shrimp. The great thing about this soup is that you can adjust the spices and seasoning. If you like it a little sweeter, add more sweetener. If you like it more spicy or sour, add those spices in. It's up to you!” - Peter Duong.
